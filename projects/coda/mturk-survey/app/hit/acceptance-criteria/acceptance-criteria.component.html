Acceptance Criteria We understand that objects can be many colors, some of which we may have never seen before. All responses will be reviewed manually, and we will do our due diligence to make sure we don't reject your work just because we weren't aware of your insightful inputs. We expect that you will be honest in your answers, and while there are no right answers, there are certainly wrong answers.

As an example, "Grass" is green. If you were assigned this object, we'd expect green to be the highest-ranking color in your annotation. While grass can be brown or yellow, these are far less common and your annotation should reflect that. However, grass is very rarely blue, red, pink, purple, etc. As such, if your response has those colors, we might reject your assignment.

An example annotation for grass would have green as 5/5, yellow 1/5, and brown 1/5. Use the entire range of the sliders to express subtle differences. Sure, certain objects can be any color, but not likely all at the same frequency. This is the type of subtlety we're looking for.

There are random control objects in every HIT. Each HIT has at least one "control object". These are objects that have known color distributions, and if your answer disagrees with them significantly, we might reject your assignment.

As a general guideline, most of the objects have a small subset of the possible colors. Very few objects should have all colors included. Select as few colors as possible. They should cover a large majority of occurrences (e.g. 90%). Rare or extraordinary instances correspond to 0 on this scale.

Note that for some objects, such as "paintings", selecting all colors or a wide distribution of colors would be appropiate.
